Legal requirements for real estate development in Tunisia include:

- Consulting legal and engineering experts to ensure all requirements are met and necessary permits are obtained.
- Obtaining a planning permit from local authorities before starting any real estate development project, including approval of the project plan and design according to urban planning laws.
- Submitting architectural and engineering designs approved by licensed professionals for approval, which must comply with building codes and environmental standards.
- Obtaining a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority after design approval, including approval of plans and commitment to building regulations.
- Conducting periodic quality inspections during the construction phase to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.
- Verifying ownership of the land used in the project and ensuring it is legally owned and properly registered in the land registry, and submitting ownership documents and legal confirmations.
- Settling property rights if the land has joint ownership or if there is a dispute regarding intellectual property rights before starting development.
- Conducting an environmental impact assessment to evaluate the project's impact on the local environment and obtaining approval from environmental authorities for certain projects.
- Obtaining special permits from relevant bodies for certain projects, such as commercial or industrial projects, like the Ministry of Industry or the Ministry of Commerce.
- Adhering to public safety and health rules, including installing safety and fire protection systems.
- Paying permit and license fees according to local regulations.
- Paying development taxes or development-related fees, such as improvement fees or property taxes.
- Registering the property in the real estate registry upon completion of construction, and submitting documents proving project completion according to legal specifications.
- Obtaining a certificate of conformity from local authorities confirming that the building meets licensing and legal requirements.
- Obtaining necessary approvals from relevant authorities, including the municipality and the land management office, if the project involves dividing land into multiple plots.
