Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to legal and administrative requirements designed to regulate the process and ensure compliance with local laws. These requirements include:

* **Project Planning and Design:** Obtaining a planning permit and approvals for architectural and engineering designs.
* **Permits and Construction:** Securing a building permit and undergoing quality inspections.
* **Land Laws Compliance:** Verifying and settling land ownership and registering the property in the land registry.  
* **Environmental Standards:** Conducting an environmental impact assessment when necessary.
* **Special Permits and Licenses:** Obtaining necessary licenses for commercial or industrial projects and complying with safety regulations.
* **Taxes and Fees:** Paying permit and license fees, and development taxes.
* **Post-Development Property Registration:** Registering the property in the land registry and obtaining a certificate of compliance.
* **Land Subdivision Compliance:** Obtaining necessary approvals for land subdivision.
