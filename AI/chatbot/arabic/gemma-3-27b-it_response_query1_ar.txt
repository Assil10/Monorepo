Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to a set of legal and administrative requirements designed to regulate the process and ensure compliance with local laws. Developers should consult with legal and engineering experts to ensure all requirements are met and necessary permits are obtained. Compliance can help avoid legal issues and expedite development.

Required legal procedures include:

* Obtaining a planning permit from local authorities before starting any project.
* Submitting architectural and engineering designs approved by licensed professionals for approval.
* Securing a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority.
* Undergoing periodic inspections during the construction phase to ensure compliance with standards and legal specifications.
* Verifying ownership and settling property rights if necessary.
* Conducting an environmental impact assessment for certain projects and obtaining approval from environmental authorities.
* Obtaining special licenses for commercial or industrial projects.
* Adhering to public safety and health regulations.
* Paying fees for permits, licenses, and taxes associated with development.
* Registering the property in the land registry and obtaining a certificate of conformity upon completion of construction.
* Obtaining the necessary approvals for land subdivision if the project involves it.
