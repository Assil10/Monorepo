Before starting any real estate development project, obtaining a planning permit from local authorities is essential. This permit signifies approval of the project plan and design, in accordance with urban planning laws.

Architectural and engineering designs, endorsed by licensed professionals, must be submitted for approval. These designs must comply with building codes and environmental standards.

Following design approval, a building permit must be obtained from the municipality or relevant authority. This permit includes approval of the blueprints and adherence to building regulations.

Environmental impact assessments, when necessary, must be approved by environmental authorities.

Certain projects, such as commercial or industrial ventures, may require specific licenses from relevant bodies, like the Ministry of Industry or Ministry of Commerce.

Land division approvals, when necessary, must be obtained from the relevant authorities, including the municipality and the Land Management Office.

A Certificate of Conformity must be obtained from local authorities upon completion of construction.

Government approval is required for land purchase.
