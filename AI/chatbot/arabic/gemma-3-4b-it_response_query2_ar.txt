Developing real estate in Tunisia requires adherence to legal and administrative requirements designed to regulate the process and ensure compliance with local laws. Developers must consult legal and engineering experts to meet all requirements and obtain necessary permits.

The requirements include:

1.  **Site Selection:** Choosing a plot of land that aligns with your needs and projects.
2.  **Property Verification:** Confirming land ownership through obtaining a property title certificate from the Real Estate Mortgage Institute.
3.  **Sales Contract Drafting:** Drafting a sales contract by a licensed notary public.
4.  **Government Approval:** Obtaining government approval to ensure the transaction complies with local laws.
5.  **Tax and Fee Payments:** Settling taxes and fees, such as registration fees and notary fees.
6.  **Land Registration:** Registering the ownership in the Land Registry.
