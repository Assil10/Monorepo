Real estate development in Tunisia necessitates adherence to legal and administrative requirements designed to regulate the process and ensure compliance with local laws. Developers should consult legal and engineering experts to secure necessary permits and meet all requirements, which can avoid legal issues and expedite development.

Project planning and design include obtaining a planning permit from local authorities, approving the project plan and design according to urban planning laws, and submitting architectural and engineering designs approved by licensed professionals that comply with building codes and environmental standards.

Licensing and construction require a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority, approving the plans and ensuring compliance with building regulations. Periodic quality inspections may be required during construction to ensure adherence to legal standards and specifications.

Land law compliance involves verifying the legal ownership of the land, ensuring it's properly registered in the land registry, and resolving any shared ownership or disputes regarding property rights before development begins.

Environmental standards may require an environmental impact assessment for certain projects to evaluate their effect on the local environment, potentially requiring approval from environmental authorities.

Special licenses and permits may be needed for projects such as commercial or industrial ventures, obtained from relevant bodies like the Ministry of Industry or the Ministry of Commerce. Compliance with public health and safety rules, including the installation of safety and fire protection systems, is essential.

Taxes and fees include permit and license fees, which must be paid according to local regulations, and development taxes or fees like improvement fees or property taxes.

Post-development property registration requires registering the property in the land registry upon completion, submitting documents proving project completion according to legal specifications, and obtaining a certificate of conformity from local authorities confirming that the construction meets licensing and legal requirements.

Compliance with land division regulations is necessary if the project involves dividing the land into multiple plots, requiring approvals from relevant authorities, including the municipality and the land management office.
