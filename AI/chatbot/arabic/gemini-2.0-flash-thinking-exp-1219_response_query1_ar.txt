The legal requirements for real estate development in Tunisia are as follows:

- A planning permit from local authorities, which includes approval of the project plan and design in accordance with urban planning laws.
- Design approval from licensed professionals, ensuring designs comply with building codes and environmental standards.
- A building permit from the municipality or competent authority, which includes approval of plans and adherence to building regulations.
- Quality inspections to verify compliance with legal standards and specifications during construction.
- Verification of land ownership for the project and ensuring it is properly registered in the land registry.
- Settlement of property rights if the land has joint ownership or if there is a dispute regarding ownership rights.
- An environmental impact assessment to evaluate the project's effect on the local environment and obtaining approval from environmental authorities if necessary.
- Special permits from relevant bodies for commercial or industrial projects.
- Compliance with public safety and health regulations, including the installation of safety and fire protection systems.
- Payment of permit and license fees according to local regulations.
- Payment of development taxes, such as betterment levies or property taxes.
- Registration of ownership in the land registry after construction completion, and submitting documents proving project completion according to legal specifications.
- Obtaining a certificate of conformity from local authorities, confirming that the construction meets licensing and legal requirements.
- Obtaining necessary approvals for land subdivision from relevant authorities, including the municipality and the land administration office, if the project involves dividing the land into multiple plots.
