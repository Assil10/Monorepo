Planning Permission: Before starting any real estate development project, obtaining a permit from local authorities is essential. This permit signifies approval of the project plan and design according to urban planning laws.

Design Approval: Architectural and engineering designs, certified by licensed professionals, must be submitted for approval.  These designs must comply with building codes and environmental standards.

Building Permit: After design approval, a building permit must be obtained from the municipality or relevant authority. This permit signifies approval of the blueprints and adherence to building regulations.

Environmental Authority Approval:  An environmental impact assessment may be required and subsequently approved by environmental authorities.

Special Permits: Certain projects, such as commercial or industrial ventures, may require specific permits from relevant bodies, such as the Ministry of Industry or Ministry of Commerce.

Certificate of Compliance/Conformity: Obtaining this certificate from local authorities confirms that the construction meets permit and legal requirements.

Land Division Approvals:  Approvals for land division may be necessary from relevant authorities, including the municipality and the Land Management Office.

Government Approval for Land Purchase:  Government approval is required for land acquisition.
