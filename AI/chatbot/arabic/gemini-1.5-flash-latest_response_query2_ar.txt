To develop real estate in Tunisia, several licenses and approvals are required:

* **Planning Permit:**  Approval from local authorities for the project plan and design, in accordance with urban planning laws.
* **Design Approval:** Approval of architectural and engineering designs certified by licensed professionals, ensuring compliance with building codes and environmental standards.
* **Building Permit:**  Permit from the municipality or relevant authority approving the plans and adherence to building regulations.
* **Environmental Impact Assessment:** (For projects requiring it) An assessment of the project's impact on the local environment, and obtaining approval from environmental authorities.
* **Special Licenses:** (For commercial or industrial projects) Specific licenses from relevant bodies, such as the Ministry of Industry or the Ministry of Commerce.
* **Government Approval:** (For land purchase) Government approval to ensure the transaction complies with local laws and the land is suitable for the intended use.
* **Title Deed:** An official document proving legal ownership of the land from the Land Registry Office (if purchasing land).
* **Land Subdivision Approvals:** (If the project involves land subdivision) Approvals from the municipality and the land management office.


Furthermore, adherence to public health and safety regulations is mandatory, including installing safety and fire protection systems.  Permit and license fees, and development taxes (such as improvement fees or property taxes) must also be paid. Finally, the property must be registered in the land registry after construction completion and obtaining a certificate of conformity from local authorities.
