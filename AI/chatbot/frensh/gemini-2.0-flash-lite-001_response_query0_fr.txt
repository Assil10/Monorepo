Legal requirements for real estate development in Tunisia include:

*   **Project Planning and Design:**
    *   Urban planning permit: Required from local authorities before starting a project, including plan and design approval according to urban planning laws.
    *   Design approval: Submission of architectural and engineering projects, certified by licensed professionals, for approval, complying with construction and environmental standards.
*   **Licenses and Construction:**
    *   Building permit: Obtaining a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority after project approval, including plan approval and adherence to building regulations.
    *   Quality control: Possible periodic inspections during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.
*   **Compliance with Land Laws:**
    *   Property verification: Ensuring the land is legally owned and registered in the land registry.
    *   Settlement of property rights: Resolving any disputes or co-ownership issues before starting development.
*   **Environmental Standards:**
    *   Environmental impact assessment: An environmental impact study may be required for certain projects, needing approval from environmental authorities.
*   **Special Licenses and Permits:**
    *   Special licenses: Obtaining specific licenses for certain commercial or industrial projects from relevant organizations.
    *   Safety standards compliance: Adherence to safety and public health rules, including the installation of safety and fire protection systems.
*   **Taxes and Fees:**
    *   Permit and license fees: Payment of fees related to obtaining permits and licenses in accordance with local regulations.
    *   Development taxes: Possible taxes or fees related to development, such as improvement fees or property taxes.
*   **Property Registration After Development:**
    *   Registration in the land registry: Registering the property in the land registry once construction is complete, requiring submission of documents proving project compliance.
    *   Certificate of compliance: Obtaining a certificate of compliance confirming that the construction meets permit and legal requirements.
*   **Compliance of Land Subdivisions:**
    *   Subdivision: Obtaining necessary authorizations for the subdivision of land into several parcels, particularly from the municipality and the land management office.

Developers must consult legal and technical experts to ensure all requirements are met and the necessary permits are obtained.
