Developing real estate in Tunisia requires obtaining various permits and authorizations, depending on the project's nature. 

**Planning and Project Design:**

* **Urban Planning Permit:** This permit, obtained from local authorities, approves the project's plan and design, ensuring compliance with urban planning laws.
* **Design Approval:** Architectural and engineering designs, certified by licensed professionals, must be submitted for approval. Designs should adhere to construction standards, codes, and environmental regulations.

**Licensing and Construction:**

* **Building Permit:** After project approval, a building permit must be obtained from the municipality or relevant authority. This permit includes approval of plans and compliance with construction regulations.
* **Quality Control:** Periodic inspections may be required during the construction phase to ensure adherence to legal standards and specifications.

**Land Law Compliance:**

* **Property Verification:** Ensure the land used for the project is legally owned and registered with the land registry.

**Environmental Standards:**

* **Environmental Impact Assessment:** For certain projects, an environmental impact assessment may be required to evaluate their impact on the local environment. This assessment may require approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* **Special Licenses:** Some projects, such as commercial or industrial developments, may require specific licenses from relevant bodies like the Ministry of Industry or the Ministry of Commerce.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

* **Subdivision:** If the project involves dividing a piece of land into multiple plots, necessary authorizations must be obtained from relevant authorities, including the municipality and the land management office.
