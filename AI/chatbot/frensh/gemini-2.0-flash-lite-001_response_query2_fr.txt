Legal requirements for real estate development in Tunisia include:

*   **Project Planning and Design:**
    *   Urban Planning Permit: A permit must be obtained from local authorities before starting any project.
    *   Design Approval: Architectural and engineering projects must be submitted for approval and comply with construction standards.
*   **Licensing and Construction:**
    *   Building Permit: A building permit must be obtained from the municipality or relevant authority.
    *   Quality Controls: Periodic inspections may be required to ensure compliance with standards.
*   **Compliance with Land Laws:**
    *   Property Verification: The land must be legally owned and registered in the land registry.
    *   Settlement of Property Rights: Resolve any disputes before starting development.
*   **Environmental Standards:**
    *   Environmental Impact Assessment: An environmental impact study may be required.
*   **Special Licenses and Permits:**
    *   Special Licenses: Certain projects may require specific licenses.
    *   Compliance with Safety Standards: Adherence to safety and public health regulations.
*   **Taxes and Fees:**
    *   Permit and License Fees: Fees may apply.
    *   Development Taxes: Taxes or fees related to development may exist.
*   **Property Registration After Development:**
    *   Registration in the Land Registry: The property must be registered in the land registry after construction.
    *   Certificate of Compliance: Obtaining a certificate of compliance.
*   **Compliance of Land Subdivisions:**
    *   Subdivision: Obtain the necessary authorizations for the subdivision of land.
