To avoid legal issues and expedite the development process, adhere to the following administrative procedures for real estate projects in Tunisia:

*   **Building Permit:** Obtain a permit from local authorities after the plan and design are approved.
*   **Design Approval:** Submit architectural and engineering projects for approval, ensuring compliance with construction and environmental standards.
*   **Building Permit:** Acquire a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority after the project is approved.
*   **Quality Controls:** Undergo periodic inspections during construction to ensure adherence to standards.
*   **Land Law Compliance:** Verify land ownership and settle any relevant property rights.
*   **Environmental Standards:** Conduct environmental impact assessments if required, obtaining approval from environmental authorities.
*   **Special Licenses:** Secure specific licenses for certain projects (commercial, industrial) from competent organizations.
*   **Safety Standards:** Comply with safety and public health regulations.
*   **Taxes and Fees:** Pay permit, license, and development fees.
*   **Property Registration:** Register property ownership with the land registry and obtain a certificate of conformity after development.
*   **Land Subdivision Permits:** Obtain necessary authorizations for dividing the land into plots (lotting).
