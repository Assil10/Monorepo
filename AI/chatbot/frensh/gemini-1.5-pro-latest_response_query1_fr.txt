Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to the following:

**Project Planning and Design:**

* **Urban Planning Permit:** Obtain a permit from local authorities approving the project plan and design, in accordance with urban planning laws.
* **Design Approval:** Submit architectural and engineering plans, certified by licensed professionals, for approval by the authorities. Designs must comply with building codes and environmental standards.

**Licensing and Construction:**

* **Building Permit:** Obtain a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority after project approval. This permit includes plan approval and adherence to building regulations.
* **Quality Controls:** Undergo periodic inspections during the construction phase to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

**Compliance with Land Laws:**

* **Property Verification:** Ensure the land is legally owned and registered in the land registry. Provide necessary property documents and legal confirmations.
* **Property Rights Settlement:** Resolve any co-ownership issues or property rights disputes before development begins.

**Environmental Standards:**

* **Environmental Impact Assessment:** Conduct an environmental impact study, if necessary, and obtain approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* **Special Licenses:** Obtain specific licenses required for commercial or industrial projects from the relevant bodies, such as the Ministry of Industry or the Ministry of Commerce.
* **Compliance with Safety Standards:** Adhere to safety and public health regulations, including installing safety and fire protection systems.

**Taxes and Fees:**

* **Permit and License Fees:** Pay fees related to obtaining permits and licenses according to local regulations.
* **Development Taxes:** Pay development-related taxes or fees, such as improvement fees or property taxes.

**Post-Development Property Registration:**

* **Land Registry Registration:** Register the property in the land registry after construction, providing documents proving the project was completed according to legal specifications.
* **Certificate of Conformity:** Obtain a certificate of conformity from local authorities confirming that the construction meets permit and legal requirements.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

* **Subdivision:** Obtain necessary authorizations for subdividing land into multiple plots from the relevant authorities, including the municipality and the land management office.
