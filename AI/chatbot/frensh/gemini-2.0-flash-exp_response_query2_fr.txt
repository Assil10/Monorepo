To develop real estate in Tunisia, the following permits and authorizations are required:

*   **Planning and Project Design:**
    *   Planning Permit: Obtained from local authorities, this includes approval of the project's plan and design in accordance with urban planning laws.
    *   Design Approval: Architectural and engineering projects, certified by licensed professionals, must be submitted for approval and comply with building and environmental standards.

*   **Licenses and Construction:**
    *   Building Permit: Obtained from the municipality or relevant authority after project approval, this includes approval of the plans and compliance with construction regulations.

*   **Environmental Standards:**
    *   Environmental Impact Assessment: Certain projects may require an environmental impact study to assess their impact on the local environment. This study may require approval from environmental authorities.

*   **Special Licenses and Permits:**
    *   Special Licenses: Projects such as commercial or industrial developments may require specific licenses from relevant bodies like the Ministry of Industry or the Ministry of Commerce.

*   **Property Registration After Development:**
    *   Certificate of Conformity: Obtaining a certificate of conformity from local authorities confirms the construction meets permit and legal requirements.

*   **Land Subdivision Compliance:**
    *   Subdivision: If the project involves subdividing land into multiple plots, necessary authorizations must be obtained from relevant authorities, including the municipality and the land management office.
