Real estate project procedures in Tunisia include:

**Planning and Design Phase:**

* Obtaining a town planning permit from local authorities, including approval of plans and project design according to urban planning laws.
* Submission and approval of architectural and engineering plans, certified by licensed professionals, conforming to building and environmental standards.

**Licensing and Construction Phase:**

* Obtaining a building permit from the municipality or competent authority, including plan approval and adherence to building regulations.
* Conducting quality controls and periodic inspections during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

**Land Aspects:**

* Verification of land ownership and registration in the land registry, providing property documents and legal confirmations.
* Settling property rights in cases of co-ownership or disputes before development begins.

**Environmental Standards:**

* Conducting an environmental impact assessment and obtaining approval from environmental authorities for certain projects, as required.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* Obtaining specific licenses from competent bodies (Ministry of Industry, Ministry of Commerce, etc.) for certain projects (commercial or industrial).
* Adhering to safety and public health standards, including the installation of safety and fire protection systems.

**Taxes and Fees:**

* Paying permit and license fees according to local regulations.
* Paying development taxes (improvement fees, property taxes, etc.).

**Post-Development Property Registration:**

* Registering the property with the land registry after construction, submitting documents proving compliance with legal specifications.
* Obtaining a certificate of conformity from local authorities.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

* Obtaining necessary authorizations from competent authorities (municipality, land management office) if the project involves land subdivision.
