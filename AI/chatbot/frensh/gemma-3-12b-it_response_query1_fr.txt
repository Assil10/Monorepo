To undertake a real estate project in Tunisia, the following administrative procedures must be followed:

**Project Planning and Design:** Secure an urban planning permit from local authorities. This includes approval of the project plan and design, adhering to urban planning laws. Submit architectural and engineering projects, certified by qualified professionals, for approval, ensuring compliance with construction standards, codes, and environmental regulations.

**Licenses and Construction:** Obtain a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority after project approval, including plan approval and adherence to construction rules. Periodic inspections may be required during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

**Land Ownership Laws:** Verify that the land used for the project is legally owned and properly registered in the land registry. Resolve any property rights issues, such as co-ownership or disputes.

**Environmental Standards:** Conduct an environmental impact study if required to assess the project's impact on the local environment and obtain approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:** Acquire specific licenses from relevant agencies (e.g., Ministry of Industry or Commerce) for certain projects, such as commercial or industrial developments. Ensure compliance with public health and safety standards, including the installation of security and fire protection systems.

**Taxes and Fees:** Pay permit and license fees as stipulated by local regulations. Pay development-related taxes or fees, such as improvement fees or property taxes.

**Property Registration Post-Development:** Register the property in the land registry once construction is complete, submitting documents proving compliance with legal specifications. Obtain a certificate of conformity from local authorities confirming that the construction meets the requirements of the permit and the law.

**Subdivision Compliance:** Obtain necessary authorizations from the municipality and the land management office if the project involves subdividing land.
