Developing a real estate project in Tunisia requires the following administrative procedures:

**Project Planning and Design:**

* **Urban Planning Permit:** Obtain a permit from local authorities approving the project plan and design, in accordance with urban planning laws.
* **Design Approval:** Submit architectural and engineering plans, certified by licensed professionals, for approval. Designs must comply with building codes and environmental standards.

**Licensing and Construction:**

* **Building Permit:** After project approval, obtain a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority, including plan approval and adherence to building regulations.
* **Quality Controls:** Undergo periodic inspections during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

**Land Law Compliance:**

* **Property Verification:** Ensure the land is legally owned and registered in the land registry.
* **Property Rights Settlement:** Resolve any co-ownership issues or property rights disputes before development begins.

**Environmental Standards:**

* **Environmental Impact Assessment:** Conduct an environmental impact assessment, if required, and obtain approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* **Special Licenses:** Obtain specific licenses for commercial or industrial projects from relevant bodies (Ministry of Industry, Ministry of Commerce).
* **Compliance with Safety Standards:** Adhere to safety and public health regulations, including installing security and fire protection systems.

**Taxes and Fees:**

* **Permit and License Fees:** Pay fees for obtaining permits and licenses.
* **Development Taxes:** Pay taxes or fees related to development (improvement fees, property taxes).

**Post-Development Property Registration:**

* **Land Registry Registration:** Register the property in the land registry after construction, providing documents proving project compliance with legal specifications.
* **Certificate of Conformity:** Obtain a certificate of conformity from local authorities confirming that the construction meets permit and legal requirements.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

* **Subdivision:** Obtain necessary authorizations for land subdivision into multiple plots from competent authorities (municipality, land management office).
