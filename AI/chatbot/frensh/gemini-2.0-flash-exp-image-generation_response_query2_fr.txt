Real estate development in Tunisia requires several permits and authorizations:

*   **Urban Planning Permit:** Required from local authorities before any project begins, involving approval of the project plan and design in accordance with urban planning laws.
*   **Design Approval:** Architectural and engineering projects, certified by accredited professionals, must be submitted for approval and must comply with construction and environmental standards.
*   **Building Permit:** Obtained from the municipality or relevant authority after project approval, encompassing plan approval and adherence to construction regulations.
*   **Special Licenses:** Certain projects, like commercial or industrial developments, may need specific licenses from relevant bodies, such as the Ministry of Industry or the Ministry of Commerce.
*   **Subdivision Authorizations:** If the project involves dividing land into multiple plots, necessary authorizations must be secured from the relevant authorities, including the municipality and the land management office.
*   **Environmental Impact Assessment:** Some projects may require an environmental impact study, which must be approved by environmental authorities.
