Administrative procedures for real estate projects in Tunisia involve several key steps:

1. Project Planning and Design:
   - Obtain a planning permit from local authorities. This is required before starting any project and involves approval of the project plan and design in accordance with urban planning laws.
   - Secure approval for architectural and engineering designs from accredited professionals. Designs must comply with construction and environmental standards.

2. Licenses and Construction:
   - Obtain a building permit from the municipality or competent authority after project approval. This permit includes plan approval and adherence to building regulations.
   - Undergo periodic quality control inspections during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

3. Compliance with Land Laws:
   - Verify land ownership for the project site and its registration in the land registry.
   - Resolve property rights if the land has co-ownership or if rights are disputed.

4. Environmental Standards:
   - Conduct an environmental impact assessment for certain projects, requiring approval from environmental authorities.

5. Special Licenses and Permits:
   - Obtain special licenses from relevant bodies, such as the Ministry of Industry or Commerce, for specific projects like commercial or industrial developments.
   - Comply with public safety and health standards, including the installation of safety and fire protection systems.

6. Taxes and Fees:
   - Pay permit and license fees according to local regulations.
   - Pay development taxes, such as improvement fees or property taxes.

7. Property Registration After Development:
   - Register the property in the land registry after construction is complete.
   - Obtain a certificate of conformity from local authorities to confirm the construction meets permit and legal requirements.

8. Land Subdivision Compliance:
   - Secure necessary authorizations from competent authorities, including the municipality and land management office, if the project involves subdividing land into multiple parcels (land development).
