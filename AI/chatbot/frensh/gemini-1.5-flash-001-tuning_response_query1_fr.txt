Real estate development in Tunisia requires compliance with a series of legal and administrative requirements. Here are the main steps to follow:

**Planning and Project Design:**

* **Building Permit:** Obtain a permit from local authorities for the approval of the project's plan and design.
* **Design Approval:** Submit architectural and engineering plans, certified by accredited professionals, for approval. Designs must comply with construction standards and environmental regulations.

**Licenses and Construction:**

* **Construction Permit:** Obtain a construction permit from the municipality or competent authority after project approval. This permit includes approval of plans and adherence to building regulations.
* **Quality Control:** Undergo periodic inspections during the construction phase to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

**Respecting Land Laws:**

* **Property Verification:** Ensure that the land used for the project is legally owned and duly registered in the land registry.
* **Property Rights Settlement:** Resolve any issues of co-ownership or property rights disputes before starting development.

**Environmental Standards:**

* **Environmental Impact Assessment:** Conduct an environmental impact assessment for certain projects to evaluate their impact on the local environment and obtain approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* **Special Licenses:** Obtain specific licenses from competent bodies for certain projects, such as commercial or industrial developments.
* **Safety Standards Compliance:** Adhere to safety and public health regulations, including the installation of security and fire protection systems.

**Taxes and Fees:**

* **Permit and License Fees:** Pay fees associated with obtaining permits and licenses in accordance with local regulations.
* **Development Taxes:** Pay taxes or fees related to development, such as improvement fees or property taxes.

**Property Registration After Development:**

* **Land Registry Registration:** Register the property in the land registry after construction by submitting documents proving that the project was completed according to legal specifications.
* **Certificate of Conformity:** Obtain a certificate of conformity from local authorities to confirm that the construction meets the requirements of the permit and the law.

**Compliance for Land Subdivision:**

* **Subdivision:** Obtain necessary authorizations from competent authorities, including the municipality and land management office, if the project involves subdividing land into multiple plots.
