Developing real estate in Tunisia requires these permits and authorizations:

**Planning and Design:**

* **Urban Planning Permit:** Obtained from local authorities before project commencement. It includes approval of the project plan and design, ensuring compliance with urban planning laws.
* **Design Approval:** Architect and engineering plans, certified by licensed professionals, must be submitted for approval, adhering to building and environmental standards.

**Licenses and Construction:**

* **Building Permit:** Secured from the municipality or relevant authority after project approval. This permit approves the plans and ensures adherence to building regulations.
* **Quality Controls:** Regular inspections during construction guarantee compliance with legal standards and specifications.

**Land Ownership Compliance:**

* **Property Verification:** Confirmation of legal land ownership and registration in the land registry.
* **Property Rights Settlement:** Resolution of any land ownership disputes before development begins.

**Environmental Standards:**

* **Environmental Impact Assessment:**  An environmental impact study may be required for certain projects, subject to approval by environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* **Special Licenses:** Specific licenses may be necessary for commercial or industrial projects, obtained from bodies like the Ministry of Industry or Commerce.
* **Compliance with Safety Standards:** Adherence to safety and public health regulations, including the installation of security and fire protection systems.

**Post-Development Property Registration:**

* **Land Registry Registration:** Property registration after construction, with proof of compliance with legal specifications.
* **Certificate of Conformity:** Obtaining a certificate of conformity from local authorities confirming the construction meets all requirements.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

* **Subdivision:** Authorizations needed for dividing land into multiple plots, obtained from the municipality and the land management office.
