Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to a series of legal and administrative requirements designed to regulate the process and ensure compliance with local laws. Developers should consult with legal and technical experts to ensure all requirements are met and necessary permits are obtained.

The main procedures include:

*   **Project Planning and Design:** Securing an urban planning permit from local authorities, including approval of the project plan and design. Designs must conform to construction and environmental standards and be certified by accredited professionals.
*   **Licensing and Construction:** Obtaining a building permit from the municipality or competent authority, including plan approval and adherence to building regulations. Periodic inspections may be required to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.
*   **Land Law Compliance:** Verifying land ownership and ensuring it is legally held and registered in the land registry. Resolving any disputed property rights.
*   **Environmental Standards:** Conducting an environmental impact study if necessary and obtaining approval from environmental authorities.
*   **Special Licenses and Permits:** Obtaining specific licenses from relevant agencies for certain projects (commercial, industrial). Ensuring compliance with public safety and health standards.
*   **Taxes and Fees:** Paying permit and license fees, as well as development taxes.
*   **Property Registration After Development:** Registering the property in the land registry and obtaining a certificate of conformity from local authorities.
*   **Compliance with Land Subdivision:** Obtaining the necessary authorizations for subdividing land into multiple parcels from the municipality and the land management office.
