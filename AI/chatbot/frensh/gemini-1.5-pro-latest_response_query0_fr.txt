Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to the following regulations:

**Project Planning and Design:**

* **Urban Planning Permit:** Mandatory before any project, this permit involves approval of the plan and design in accordance with urban planning laws.
* **Design Approval:** Architectural and engineering plans, certified by licensed professionals, must be submitted for approval and comply with building and environmental standards.

**Licensing and Construction:**

* **Building Permit:** Required after project approval, this permit includes plan approval and adherence to building regulations.
* **Quality Controls:** Regular inspections during construction ensure compliance with legal standards.

**Compliance with Land Laws:**

* **Property Verification:** The land must be legally owned and registered in the land registry.
* **Property Rights Settlement:** Any disputes or joint ownership must be resolved before development begins.

**Environmental Standards:**

* **Environmental Impact Assessment:** An impact study may be required for certain projects, with approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* **Special Licenses:** Specific licenses may be required for commercial or industrial projects.
* **Compliance with Safety Standards:** Adherence to safety and public health regulations, including the installation of security and fire protection systems.

**Taxes and Fees:**

* **Permit and License Fees:** Payment of fees related to permits and licenses according to local regulations.
* **Development Taxes:** Taxes or fees related to development, such as improvement fees or property taxes.

**Post-Development Property Registration:**

* **Land Registry Registration:** The property must be registered in the land registry after construction.
* **Certificate of Conformity:** A certificate of conformity from local authorities confirms that the construction meets legal requirements.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

* **Subdivision:**  Authorizations are required for the subdivision of land into multiple plots, from the municipality and the land management office.
