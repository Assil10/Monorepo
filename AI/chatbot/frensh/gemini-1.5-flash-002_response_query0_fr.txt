Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to numerous legal and administrative requirements to regulate the process and ensure compliance with local laws.  Key steps include:

**Project Planning and Design:**

* **Urban Planning Permit:** Obtain a permit from local authorities for project plan and design approval, in accordance with urban planning laws.
* **Design Approval:** Submit architectural and engineering plans (certified by licensed professionals) for approval, ensuring compliance with building codes, standards, and environmental regulations.

**Licenses and Construction:**

* **Building Permit:** Obtain a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority after project approval.
* **Quality Control:** Undergo periodic inspections during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

**Land Ownership Compliance:**

* **Property Verification:** Ensure the land is legally owned and registered with the land registry, providing necessary ownership documents and legal confirmations.
* **Property Rights Settlement:** Resolve any disputes or co-ownership issues concerning the land before development begins.

**Environmental Standards:**

* **Environmental Impact Assessment:** Conduct an environmental impact assessment (if required) and obtain approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* **Special Licenses:** Obtain specific licenses from relevant bodies (e.g., Ministry of Industry or Commerce) for certain projects (commercial or industrial).
* **Safety Standards Compliance:** Adhere to public safety and health regulations, including installing safety and fire protection systems.

**Taxes and Fees:**

* **Permit and License Fees:** Pay fees associated with obtaining permits and licenses according to local regulations.
* **Development Taxes:** Pay development-related taxes or fees (improvement fees or property taxes).

**Post-Development Property Registration:**

* **Land Registry Registration:** Register the property with the land registry after construction, submitting documents proving compliance with legal specifications.
* **Certificate of Conformity:** Obtain a certificate of conformity from local authorities.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

* **Subdivision:** Obtain necessary authorizations from competent authorities (municipality and land management office) if the project involves land subdivision.

Developers should consult legal and technical experts to ensure compliance with all requirements, avoiding legal issues and accelerating the development process.
