For real estate development in Tunisia, the following permits and authorizations are necessary:

*   **Urban Planning Permit:** Required from local authorities before starting any project. This includes approval of the project's plan and design in accordance with urban planning laws.

*   **Design Approval:** Architectural and engineering projects, certified by accredited professionals, must be submitted for approval. Designs must comply with construction and environmental standards.

*   **Building Permit:** Required from the municipality or competent authority after project approval. This includes approval of the plans and compliance with building regulations.

*   **Environmental Impact Assessment:** For certain projects, an environmental impact study may be required and must be approved by environmental authorities.

*   **Special Licenses:** Certain projects, such as commercial or industrial developments, may require specific licenses from competent bodies, such as the Ministry of Industry or the Ministry of Commerce.

*   **Subdivision Authorizations:** If the project involves subdividing land, the necessary authorizations must be obtained from the relevant authorities, including the municipality and the land management office.

*   **Certificate of Conformity:** Required from local authorities upon completion of construction, confirming that the construction meets the requirements of the permit and the law.
