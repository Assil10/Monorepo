Real estate development in Tunisia requires adhering to several legal and administrative requirements designed to regulate the process and ensure compliance with local laws.

**Project Planning and Design:**

*   An urban planning permit is required from local authorities, including approval of the project's plan and design in accordance with urban planning laws.
*   Architectural and engineering designs, certified by accredited professionals, must be submitted for approval and comply with construction and environmental standards.

**Licenses and Construction:**

*   A building permit must be obtained from the municipality or competent authority after project approval, including approval of plans and compliance with construction regulations.
*   Periodic inspections may be required during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

**Compliance with Land Laws:**

*   Verification that the land used for the project is legally owned and duly registered in the land registry, with provision of ownership documents and legal confirmations, is essential.
*   Resolution of any co-ownership or contested land rights issues must occur before development begins.

**Environmental Standards:**

*   For certain projects, an environmental impact assessment may be required and necessitate approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

*   Specific licenses may be needed from relevant bodies (Ministry of Industry or Commerce) for certain projects, such as commercial or industrial developments.
*   Compliance with safety and public health standards is necessary, including installing safety and fire protection systems.

**Taxes and Fees:**

*   Payment of fees related to obtaining permits and licenses is required in accordance with local regulations.
*   Payment of development-related taxes or fees (improvement fees or property taxes) is mandatory.

**Property Registration After Development:**

*   Registration of the property in the land registry must occur after construction is complete, with submission of documents proving project compliance.
*   A certificate of conformity must be obtained from local authorities, confirming that the construction meets permit and legal requirements.

**Compliance of Land Subdivisions:**

*   If the project involves subdividing land into multiple plots, obtaining necessary authorizations from the relevant authorities (municipality and land management office) is essential.
