Developing property in Tunisia requires adherence to a series of legal and administrative requirements designed to regulate the process and ensure compliance with local laws. Developers must consult legal and technical experts to confirm all requirements are met and necessary permits are obtained. Compliance with these requirements can help avoid legal issues and expedite the development process.

Specifically, the requirements include:

*   Building permits
*   Design approval (compliance with construction and environmental standards)
*   Building permits
*   Quality control
*   Property verification and resolution of property rights
*   Environmental compliance (environmental impact assessment)
*   Special licenses (for certain commercial or industrial projects)
*   Safety standards compliance
*   Permit and license fees
*   Development taxes
*   Registration in the land registry
*   Certificate of compliance
*   Compliance of land subdivisions
