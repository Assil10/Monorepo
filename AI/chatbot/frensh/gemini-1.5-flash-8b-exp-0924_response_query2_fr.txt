To develop real estate in Tunisia, you need:

* **Urban planning permit:** for approval of the project plan and design.
* **Design approval:** of architectural and engineering projects, certified by licensed professionals.
* **Building permit:** after project approval, for approval of construction plans and compliance with building regulations.
* **Property verification:** to ensure the land is legally owned and registered with the land registry.
* **Settlement of property rights:** if the land has co-ownership or disputed rights.
* **Environmental impact study:** for some projects.
* **Special licenses:** for some commercial or industrial projects.
* **Compliance with safety standards:** for installation of security and fire protection systems.
* **Land registry registration:** once construction is complete.
* **Certificate of compliance:** to confirm the construction meets permit and legal requirements.
* **Necessary authorizations:** for land subdivision if the project involves dividing a plot of land.
