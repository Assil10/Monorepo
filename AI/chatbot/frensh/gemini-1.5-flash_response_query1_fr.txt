Tunisian real estate project administrative procedures involve several phases:

**Planning and Design Phase:**

* Obtain a town planning permit from local authorities, including plan and design approval according to urban planning laws.
* Obtain approval for architectural and engineering designs, certified by licensed professionals, conforming to building codes, standards, and environmental regulations.

**Licensing and Construction Phase:**

* Obtain a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority, including plan approval and adherence to building regulations.
* Undergo quality control and periodic inspections during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

**Land Ownership Compliance:**

* Verify legal land ownership and registration with the land registry, providing necessary property documents and legal confirmations.
* Resolve any property rights disputes before development begins.

**Environmental Standards:**

* Conduct an environmental impact assessment if necessary and obtain approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* Obtain specific licenses from competent bodies (Ministry of Industry or Commerce) for certain projects (commercial or industrial).
* Comply with public health and safety standards, including fire safety and security systems.

**Taxes and Fees:**

* Pay permit and license fees according to local regulations.
* Pay development taxes, such as improvement fees or property taxes.

**Post-Development Property Registration:**

* Register the property with the land registry after construction, submitting documents proving compliance with legal specifications.
* Obtain a certificate of conformity from local authorities.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

* Obtain necessary authorizations from competent authorities (municipality and land management office) if the project involves land subdivision.
