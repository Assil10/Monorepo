Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to legal and administrative requirements designed to regulate the process and ensure compliance with local laws. Developers should consult legal and technical experts to ensure all requirements are met and necessary permits are obtained.

Legal requirements include:

*   **Project Planning and Design:** Obtaining a building permit from local authorities for project plan and design approval; approval of architectural and engineering designs certified by accredited professionals, in accordance with construction and environmental standards.
*   **Licenses and Construction:** Obtaining a construction permit from the municipality or competent authority after project approval; periodic inspections during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.
*   **Land Law Compliance:** Verification of land ownership used for the project and its registration in the land registry; settlement of property rights in cases of co-ownership or disputes.
*   **Environmental Standards:** Completion of an environmental impact assessment for certain projects and approval by environmental authorities.
*   **Special Licenses and Permits:** Obtaining specific licenses for certain projects (commercial, industrial) from relevant bodies (Ministry of Industry, Ministry of Commerce); compliance with safety and public health rules, including the installation of safety and fire protection systems.
*   **Taxes and Fees:** Payment of permit and license fees according to local regulations; payment of development taxes (improvement fees, property taxes).
*   **Property Registration After Development:** Registration of property in the land registry after construction, with submission of documents proving project compliance with legal specifications; obtaining a certificate of conformity from local authorities.
*   **Compliance of Land Subdivision:** Obtaining the necessary authorizations from the competent authorities (municipality, land management office) for the subdivision of land into several plots.
