Legal requirements for real estate development in Tunisia include:

*   **Project Planning and Design:**
    *   Urban Planning Permit: Obtaining a permit from local authorities, including approval of the project plan and design in accordance with urban planning laws.
    *   Design Approval: Submission and approval of architectural and engineering projects, certified by licensed professionals, complying with construction standards.
*   **Licenses and Construction:**
    *   Building Permit: Obtaining a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority, including plan approval and adherence to construction regulations.
    *   Quality Controls: Periodic inspections to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.
*   **Compliance with Land Laws:**
    *   Property Verification: Ensuring the land is legally owned and registered in the land registry.
    *   Property Rights Settlement: Resolving property rights in cases of co-ownership or disputes.
*   **Environmental Standards:**
    *   Environmental Impact Assessment: An environmental impact study may be required for certain projects.
*   **Special Licenses and Permits:**
    *   Special Licenses: Obtaining specific licenses for certain projects (commercial or industrial) from the relevant organizations.
    *   Compliance with Safety Standards: Adhering to safety and public health regulations.
*   **Taxes and Fees:**
    *   Permit and License Fees: Payment of fees related to obtaining permits and licenses.
    *   Development Taxes: Payment of taxes or fees related to development.
*   **Property Registration After Development:**
    *   Registration in the Land Registry: Registering the property in the land registry after construction.
    *   Certificate of Compliance: Obtaining a certificate of compliance confirming that the construction meets permit and legal requirements.
*   **Compliance of Land Subdivisions:**
    *   Subdivision: Obtaining the necessary authorizations for dividing a land into multiple parcels.
