Real estate development in Tunisia requires compliance with local laws.

**Project Planning and Design:**

* **Planning Permit:** Required before starting development, including project plan and design approval.
* **Design Approval:**  Certified architectural and engineering designs, complying with building codes and environmental standards, are needed.

**Licensing and Construction:**

* **Building Permit:** Obtained from the municipality after design approval, ensuring plan approval and adherence to regulations.
* **Quality Inspections:** Regular inspections during construction to ensure compliance.

**Compliance with Land Laws:**

* **Property Verification:**  Verify legal ownership and registration in the land registry.
* **Settlement of Property Rights:** Address any joint ownership or disputes before development.

**Environmental Standards:**

* **Environmental Impact Assessment:** Some projects require an assessment of environmental impact and approval from relevant authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* **Special Licenses:** Certain projects (e.g., commercial, industrial) may require specific licenses from relevant ministries.
* **Safety Compliance:** Adherence to safety and public health regulations.

**Taxation and Fees:**

* **Permit and Licensing Fees:** Fees are based on local regulations.
* **Development Taxes:** Possible development-related taxes.

**Property Registration Post-Development:**

* **Land Registry Registration:** Property must be registered in the land registry after completion.
* **Compliance Certificate:** Confirmation from local authorities that construction meets requirements.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

* **Land Subdivision:** Necessary approvals from relevant authorities are required for land subdivisions.
