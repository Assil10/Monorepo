Tunisian real estate development requires adherence to legal and administrative procedures. Developers should consult legal and engineering experts to ensure compliance and obtain necessary permits.

**Project Planning and Design:**

*   A planning permit from local authorities is required, including approval of the project plan and design based on urban planning laws.
*   Architectural and engineering designs, certified by licensed professionals, must be submitted for approval and comply with building codes and environmental standards.

**Licensing and Construction:**

*   A building permit must be obtained from the municipality or relevant authority, with approved plans and adherence to building regulations.
*   Periodic inspections may occur during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

**Compliance with Land Laws:**

*   Verify the land used is legally owned and registered in the land registry.
*   Resolve joint ownership or disputes before development commences.

**Environmental Standards:**

*   Some projects require an environmental impact assessment, needing approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

*   Certain projects, like commercial or industrial developments, may require specific licenses from relevant bodies, such as the Ministry of Industry or Commerce.
*   Compliance with safety and public health regulations is necessary, including safety and fire protection systems.

**Taxation and Fees:**

*   Fees apply for obtaining permits and licenses.
*   Development-related taxes or fees, such as improvement fees or property taxes, may exist.

**Property Registration Post-Development:**

*   Register the property in the land registry, submitting documents proving completion according to legal specifications.
*   Obtain a compliance certificate from local authorities confirming construction meets permit and legal requirements.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

*   If subdividing land into multiple plots, obtain necessary approvals from relevant authorities, including the municipality and land management office.

**Government Regulation:**

*   Land Use and Urban Planning Law governs urban planning and land development, including regulations on zoning, land subdivisions, and infrastructure.
*   Environmental Protection Law aims to ensure adherence to environmental standards.
*   An environmental license may be required for major projects, assessing the project’s environmental impact.
*   Local authorities prepare guiding plans that define land use.
*   Developers may need to submit impact studies covering the project’s environmental, social, and economic effects for large-scale projects.
*   Local authorities monitor construction projects to ensure compliance.
*   Projects are inspected for compliance with environmental standards.
