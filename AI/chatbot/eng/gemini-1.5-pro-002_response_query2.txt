Planning permits must be obtained from local authorities before development begins. This includes project plan and design approval according to urban planning laws.  Architectural and engineering designs, certified by licensed professionals, must be submitted for approval and comply with building codes and environmental standards.

A building permit is obtained from the municipality or relevant authority after design approval. This includes approval of plans and adherence to building regulations.  Periodic quality inspections occur during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

Land ownership and proper registration in the land registry must be verified. Any joint ownership or disputed land rights should be resolved before development begins.

An environmental impact assessment is required for some projects to evaluate environmental impact and may need approval from environmental authorities.

Specific licenses may be required for commercial or industrial developments from relevant bodies like the Ministry of Industry or Ministry of Commerce. Safety and public health regulations, including safety and fire protection systems, must be adhered to.

Fees apply for obtaining permits and licenses. Development-related taxes or fees, such as improvement fees or property taxes, may also apply.

The property must be registered in the land registry upon completion of construction. A compliance certificate must be obtained from local authorities confirming construction meets permit and legal requirements.

Approvals for land subdivision projects are required from relevant authorities, including the municipality and land management office.
