Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to legal and administrative regulations.  Developers must consult legal and engineering experts.

**Project Planning and Design:**

* **Planning Permit:**  A permit from local authorities is required, encompassing project plan and design approval per urban planning laws.
* **Design Approval:**  Certified architectural and engineering designs, complying with building codes and environmental standards, must be approved.

**Licensing and Construction:**

* **Building Permit:**  A building permit from the municipality or relevant authority is needed, approving plans and adherence to building regulations.
* **Quality Inspections:**  Periodic inspections during construction ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

**Compliance with Land Laws:**

* **Property Verification:**  The land's legal ownership and registration in the land registry must be confirmed.  Property documents and legal confirmations may be required.
* **Settlement of Property Rights:**  Any joint ownership or disputed rights must be resolved before development begins.

**Environmental Standards:**

* **Environmental Impact Assessment:**  Some projects require an environmental impact assessment, with approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* **Special Licenses:**  Certain projects (commercial, industrial) may require specific licenses from relevant ministries (e.g., Industry, Commerce).
* **Safety Compliance:**  Compliance with safety and public health regulations, including safety and fire protection systems, is necessary.

**Taxation and Fees:**

* **Permit and Licensing Fees:**  Fees for permits and licenses are payable according to local regulations.
* **Development Taxes:**  Development-related taxes or fees (improvement fees, property taxes) may apply.

**Property Registration Post-Development:**

* **Land Registry Registration:**  Completed properties must be registered in the land registry, requiring documents proving legal completion.
* **Compliance Certificate:**  A compliance certificate from local authorities confirms project completion and compliance.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

* **Land Subdivision:**  If the project involves land subdivision, approvals from relevant authorities (municipality, land management office) are required.
