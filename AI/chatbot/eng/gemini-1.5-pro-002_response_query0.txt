Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to legal and administrative requirements. Developers should consult legal and engineering experts.

**Project Planning and Design:**  A planning permit from local authorities is required before development, including project plan and design approval. Architectural and engineering designs, certified by licensed professionals, must be submitted for approval, adhering to building codes and environmental standards.

**Licensing and Construction:** A building permit is required after design approval.  Periodic inspections during construction ensure compliance.

**Compliance with Land Laws:** Verify legal ownership and registration of the land. Resolve any joint ownership or disputes beforehand.

**Environmental Standards:** Some projects require an environmental impact assessment, potentially needing approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:** Commercial or industrial projects may require specific licenses from bodies like the Ministry of Industry or Commerce. Safety and public health regulations must be followed, including safety and fire protection systems.

**Taxation and Fees:** Permit and licensing fees apply. Development-related taxes, like improvement or property taxes, may also exist.

**Property Registration Post-Development:** Upon completion, the property must be registered in the land registry with proof of legal completion. A compliance certificate from local authorities is also required.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:** If subdividing land, obtain necessary approvals from authorities, including the municipality and land management office.
