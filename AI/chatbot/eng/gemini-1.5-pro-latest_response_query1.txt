Obtaining a Planning Permit from local authorities is essential, including project plan and design approval according to urban planning laws.  Architectural and engineering designs, certified by licensed professionals, must be submitted for approval, ensuring compliance with building codes and environmental standards.

A Building Permit from the municipality or relevant authority is required after design approval. This includes plan approval and adherence to building regulations.  Periodic Quality Inspections during construction are mandatory to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

Property ownership must be verified and properly registered in the land registry, providing necessary property documents and legal confirmations.  Any Property Rights disputes or joint ownership issues should be settled before development begins.

An Environmental Impact Assessment may be required for some projects to evaluate its effect on the local environment and obtain approval from environmental authorities.

Special Licenses for certain projects (commercial or industrial) must be obtained from relevant bodies, such as the Ministry of Industry or the Ministry of Commerce. Safety Compliance with public health regulations, including safety and fire protection systems installation, is also required.

Permit and Licensing Fees must be paid according to local regulations, along with any applicable Development Taxes or fees, such as improvement fees or property taxes.

Upon completion of construction, the property must be registered in the Land Registry, submitting documents proving project completion according to legal specifications. A Compliance Certificate from local authorities confirming that the construction meets permit and legal requirements must also be obtained.

For land subdivision, necessary approvals must be obtained from relevant authorities, including the municipality and land management office.
