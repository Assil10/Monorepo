Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to the following legal requirements:

**Project Planning and Design:**

*   A planning permit must be obtained from local authorities before starting any real estate development, approving the project plan and design in accordance with urban planning laws.
*   Architectural and engineering designs, certified by licensed professionals, must be submitted for approval, ensuring compliance with building codes and environmental standards.

**Licensing and Construction:**

*   A building permit must be obtained from the municipality or relevant authority after design approval, confirming plan adherence and compliance with building regulations.
*   Periodic inspections may be required during construction to ensure adherence to legal standards and specifications.

**Compliance with Land Laws:**

*   The land used for the project must be legally owned and properly registered in the land registry, requiring property documents and legal confirmations.
*   Any joint ownership or disputed rights must be resolved before development begins.

**Environmental Standards:**

*   An environmental impact assessment may be required for certain projects to evaluate their environmental effects, potentially requiring approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

*   Specific licenses may be required for projects like commercial or industrial developments from bodies such as the Ministry of Industry or the Ministry of Commerce.
*   Compliance with safety and public health regulations, including safety and fire protection system installations, is mandatory.

**Taxation and Fees:**

*   Fees related to obtaining permits and licenses must be paid according to local regulations.
*   Development-related taxes or fees, such as improvement fees or property taxes, may apply.

**Property Registration Post-Development:**

*   Upon completion of construction, the property must be registered in the land registry, supported by documents proving legal compliance.
*   A compliance certificate must be obtained from local authorities, confirming the construction meets permit and legal requirements.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

*   Necessary approvals, including those from the municipality and the land management office, must be obtained if the project involves subdividing land into multiple plots.
