Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to legal and administrative procedures, necessitating consultation with legal and engineering experts.

Project planning and design demand a planning permit from local authorities for project plan and design approval, according to urban planning laws. Architectural and engineering designs, certified by licensed professionals, must be submitted and comply with building codes and environmental standards.

Licensing and construction require a building permit from the municipality after design approval, ensuring adherence to building regulations. Periodic quality inspections may be necessary during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

Land laws dictate property verification, ensuring legal ownership and registration in the land registry, potentially requiring property documents and legal confirmations. Any joint ownership or property disputes must be resolved before development.

Environmental standards may require an environmental impact assessment for some projects, needing approval from environmental authorities.

Special licenses may be necessary for commercial or industrial developments from bodies like the Ministry of Industry or Ministry of Commerce. Compliance with safety and public health regulations, including safety and fire protection systems, is mandatory.

Taxation and fees apply to permits and licenses, paid according to local regulations. Development-related taxes or fees, such as improvement fees or property taxes, may also apply.

Post-development, land registry registration is required, submitting documents proving legal completion. A compliance certificate from local authorities confirms the construction meets permit and legal requirements.

Land subdivision projects require approvals from authorities like the municipality and land management office.
