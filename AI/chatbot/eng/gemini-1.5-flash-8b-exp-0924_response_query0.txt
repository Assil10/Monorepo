Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to local laws.  Developers must consult legal and engineering experts.

**Project Planning and Design:**

* **Planning Permit:** Obtain a permit from local authorities for project plan and design approval.
* **Design Approval:** Submit certified architectural and engineering designs, complying with building codes and environmental standards, for approval.

**Licensing and Construction:**

* **Building Permit:** Obtain a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority.
* **Quality Inspections:** Periodic inspections during construction to ensure compliance.

**Compliance with Land Laws:**

* **Property Verification:** Verify legal ownership and registration in the land registry.
* **Settlement of Property Rights:** Resolve joint ownership or disputes before development.

**Environmental Standards:**

* **Environmental Impact Assessment:** Some projects may require an environmental impact assessment.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* **Special Licenses:** Certain projects (commercial/industrial) may require specific licenses from relevant ministries.
* **Safety Compliance:** Comply with safety and public health regulations.

**Taxation and Fees:**

* **Permit and Licensing Fees:** Pay fees for permits and licenses.
* **Development Taxes:** Pay development-related taxes.

**Property Registration Post-Development:**

* **Land Registry Registration:** Register the property in the land registry.
* **Compliance Certificate:** Obtain a compliance certificate from local authorities.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

* **Land Subdivision:** Obtain necessary approvals from relevant authorities for land subdivision projects.
