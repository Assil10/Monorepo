Project planning and design involves obtaining a planning permit from local authorities. This includes project plan and design approval, adhering to urban planning laws.  Architectural and engineering designs must receive approval from licensed professionals and comply with building codes and environmental standards.

Licensing and construction require a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority, encompassing plan approval and adherence to building regulations.  Quality inspections are conducted throughout construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

Compliance with land laws necessitates property verification to confirm legal ownership and proper registration in the land registry.  Any existing joint ownership or disputes regarding property rights must be settled.

Some projects require an environmental impact assessment and subsequent approval from environmental authorities.

Special licenses are needed for specific commercial or industrial projects from relevant bodies such as the Ministry of Industry or Ministry of Commerce.  Safety compliance with regulations concerning public health and safety, including fire protection systems, is mandatory.

Permit and licensing fees are payable according to local regulations.  Development taxes, including improvement fees or property taxes, also apply.

Post-development property registration involves registering the completed construction with the land registry, providing documents that prove completion according to legal specifications.  A compliance certificate from local authorities confirming adherence to permit and legal requirements is also necessary.

Land subdivision projects require approvals from relevant authorities, including the municipality and land management office.
