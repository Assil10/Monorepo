Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to these legal and administrative requirements:

**Project Planning and Design:**

* **Planning Permit:**  Needed before starting, including project plan and design approval per urban planning laws.
* **Design Approval:**  Architectural and engineering designs, certified by licensed professionals, must be approved.  Designs must comply with building codes and environmental standards.

**Licensing and Construction:**

* **Building Permit:**  Obtained from the municipality or relevant authority after design approval, including plan approval and adherence to building regulations.
* **Quality Inspections:**  Periodic inspections during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

**Compliance with Land Laws:**

* **Property Verification:**  Ensure land ownership and proper registration in the land registry, requiring property documents and legal confirmations.
* **Settlement of Property Rights:**  Resolve any joint ownership or disputed rights before starting development.

**Environmental Standards:**

* **Environmental Impact Assessment:**  Required for some projects to evaluate environmental effect, requiring approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* **Special Licenses:** Certain projects (commercial or industrial, for example) may require specific licenses from relevant bodies (Ministry of Industry, Ministry of Commerce).
* **Safety Compliance:**  Compliance with safety and public health regulations, including safety and fire protection systems.

**Taxation and Fees:**

* **Permit and Licensing Fees:**  Fees for permits and licenses, paid according to local regulations.
* **Development Taxes:** Potential development-related taxes or fees (improvement fees, property taxes, for example).

**Property Registration Post-Development:**

* **Land Registry Registration:**  Register the property in the land registry upon completion, requiring completion documents according to legal specifications.
* **Compliance Certificate:**  Obtain a compliance certificate from local authorities confirming construction meets permit and legal requirements.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

* **Land Subdivision:**  If the project involves subdividing land, necessary approvals must be obtained from relevant authorities (municipality, land management office).
