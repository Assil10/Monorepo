Real estate development in Tunisia requires adherence to legal and administrative regulations.  Consult legal and engineering experts.

**Project Planning and Design:**

* **Planning Permit:** Required before starting, including project plan and design approval.
* **Design Approval:** Architectural and engineering designs, certified by licensed professionals, must be approved. Designs must comply with building codes and environmental standards.

**Licensing and Construction:**

* **Building Permit:** Required after design approval, including plan approval and adherence to building regulations.
* **Quality Inspections:** Periodic inspections during construction to ensure compliance.

**Compliance with Land Laws:**

* **Property Verification:** Ensure land is legally owned and registered.
* **Settlement of Property Rights:** Resolve any joint ownership or disputed rights.

**Environmental Standards:**

* **Environmental Impact Assessment:** Required for some projects to evaluate environmental effect and obtain approval from environmental authorities.

**Special Licenses and Permits:**

* **Special Licenses:** Certain projects may require specific licenses (e.g., Ministry of Industry, Ministry of Commerce).
* **Safety Compliance:** Compliance with safety and public health regulations.

**Taxation and Fees:**

* **Permit and Licensing Fees:** Fees for permits and licenses must be paid.
* **Development Taxes:** Development-related taxes or fees may apply.

**Property Registration Post-Development:**

* **Land Registry Registration:** Property must be registered upon completion.
* **Compliance Certificate:** Obtaining a certificate confirming construction meets requirements.

**Land Subdivision Compliance:**

* **Land Subdivision:** If applicable, necessary approvals must be obtained.
