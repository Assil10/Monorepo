Real estate development in Tunisia requires compliance with these legal and administrative requirements:

**Legal and Administrative Requirements:**

* **Project Planning and Design:** Obtain a planning permit, including project plan and design approval, adhering to urban planning laws.  Submit certified architectural and engineering designs complying with building codes and environmental standards.
* **Licensing and Construction:** Obtain a building permit, including plan approval and adherence to building regulations.  Undergo periodic construction inspections to ensure compliance.
* **Compliance with Land Laws:** Ensure land ownership is legally registered and properly documented. Resolve any joint ownership or disputed rights.
* **Environmental Standards:**  Some projects may require an environmental impact assessment and approval.
* **Special Licenses and Permits:** Certain projects (e.g., commercial, industrial) may require specific licenses from relevant ministries.  Adhere to safety and public health regulations.
* **Taxation and Fees:** Pay fees for permits and licenses, and potential development-related taxes or fees (e.g., improvement fees, property taxes).
* **Property Registration Post-Development:** Register the completed property in the land registry with proof of completion. Obtain a compliance certificate from local authorities.
* **Land Subdivision Compliance:** Obtain necessary approvals if the project involves land subdivision.
