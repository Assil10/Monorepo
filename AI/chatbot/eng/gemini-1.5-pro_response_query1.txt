Obtain a planning permit from local authorities, including project plan and design approval per urban planning laws.  Submit architectural and engineering designs, certified by licensed professionals, for approval. Designs must comply with building codes and environmental standards.

Obtain a building permit from the municipality or relevant authority after design approval. This includes plan approval and adherence to building regulations.  Undergo periodic inspections during construction to ensure compliance with legal standards and specifications.

Ensure land ownership and proper registration in the land registry. Provide property documents and legal confirmations. Resolve any joint ownership or disputed land rights before development.

Conduct an environmental impact assessment for some projects and obtain approval from environmental authorities.

Obtain specific licenses for certain projects (commercial or industrial) from relevant bodies (Ministry of Industry or Ministry of Commerce). Comply with safety and public health regulations, including safety and fire protection systems installation.

Pay fees for permits and licenses according to local regulations. Pay development-related taxes or fees (improvement fees or property taxes).

Register the property in the land registry upon construction completion, submitting documents proving project completion according to legal specifications. Obtain a compliance certificate from local authorities, confirming construction meets permit and legal requirements.

Obtain necessary approvals for land subdivision projects from relevant authorities, including the municipality and land management office.
